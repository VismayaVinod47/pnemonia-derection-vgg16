Common Warning Signs

Watch for persistent cough with phlegm, rapid breathing, chest pain while breathing, fever, fatigue, and low-oxygen symptoms. These signs can indicate possible lung infection and should be evaluated early.

Awareness Guide

When To Seek Medical Evaluation

01

Observe Symptoms

Monitor breathing effort, fever duration, cough severity, and unusual fatigue for more than a few days.

02

Prioritize High-Risk Cases

Immediate evaluation is recommended for children, seniors, or anyone with low oxygen signs and chest discomfort.

03

Use Screening Responsibly

AI chest X-ray output should support awareness and triage, not replace physician diagnosis or treatment planning.

Prevention Focus

Daily Steps That Reduce Risk

Respiratory Hygiene

Wash hands regularly, cover coughs and sneezes, and avoid close exposure to people with active respiratory infections.

Immunity Support

Maintain sleep, nutrition, hydration, and manage existing conditions such as asthma, diabetes, or chronic heart and lung disease.

Timely Follow-up

If symptoms worsen despite medication, seek re-evaluation quickly and do not delay chest imaging when advised.
